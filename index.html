Battery Safety

  • ALWAYS use LiPo-safe bags for storage

‚öôÔ∏è Understanding Drone Physics

The Four Forces of Flight

1. THRUST ‚¨ÜÔ∏è

Definition: Force generated by propellers pushing air downward

How it works: Spinning propellers accelerate air molecules downward. By Newton's 3rd Law (action-reaction), this pushes the drone upward.

Control: Motor speed controlled via ESCs

2. WEIGHT (Gravity) ‚¨áÔ∏è

Definition: Force pulling drone toward Earth's center

Formula: Weight = Mass √ó Gravity (9.8 m/s¬≤)

Effect: Must be overcome by thrust for flight

3. LIFT ‚¨ÜÔ∏è

Definition: In quadcopters, lift = combined thrust from all propellers

Flight States:

  • Lift > Weight ‚Üí Drone ascends
  • Lift = Weight ‚Üí Drone hovers
  • Lift < Weight ‚Üí Drone descends

4. DRAG ‚Üê

Definition: Air resistance opposing motion

Factors: Speed, surface area, air density

Minimized by: Aerodynamic frame design

1. THROTTLE (Altitude Control)

What it does: Makes drone go up or down

How it works: All four motors increase/decrease speed equally

2. ROLL (Left/Right Tilt)

What it does: Tilts and moves sideways

How it works: Opposite diagonal motors change speed differently

Roll Right Example:
Motor 1 (FR): 1500 ‚Üí 1300 RPM ‚¨áÔ∏è
Motor 2 (RL): 1500 ‚Üí 1300 RPM ‚¨áÔ∏è
Motor 3 (FL): 1500 ‚Üí 1700 RPM ‚¨ÜÔ∏è
Motor 4 (RR): 1500 ‚Üí 1700 RPM ‚¨ÜÔ∏è
Result: Drone tilts right, moves right

3. PITCH (Forward/Backward Tilt)

What it does: Tilts forward or backward

How it works: Front and rear motors adjust oppositely

Pitch Forward Example:
Motor 1 (FR): 1500 ‚Üí 1300 RPM ‚¨áÔ∏è
Motor 3 (FL): 1500 ‚Üí 1300 RPM ‚¨áÔ∏è
Motor 2 (RL): 1500 ‚Üí 1700 RPM ‚¨ÜÔ∏è
Motor 4 (RR): 1500 ‚Üí 1700 RPM ‚¨ÜÔ∏è
Result: Drone tilts forward, moves forward

4. YAW (Rotation)

What it does: Spins left or right (changes heading)

How it works: Uses Newton's 3rd Law and propeller torque

Yaw Right Example:
CW Motors (1,2): Speed up ‚¨ÜÔ∏è
CCW Motors (3,4): Slow down ‚¨áÔ∏è
Result: Drone body rotates clockwise

Why it works: Propellers create counter-torque. When CW motors spin faster, they create more CCW torque on the drone body, causing it to rotate CW.

Key Principle: The flight controller makes micro-adjustments to motor speeds hundreds of times per second to maintain stable flight while executing pilot commands.

2. Brushless Motors (4x) - ~$40-60 total

Model: 920KV or 1000KV

What KV means: RPM per volt (920KV = 920 RPM per 1V)

Specifications:

  • Voltage: 11.1V (3S LiPo)
  • Current Draw: 10-15A at full throttle
  • Thrust: 800-1000g per motor
  • Weight: ~50g each

How They Work:

  • 3 wire coils (windings) inside - Phase A, B, C
  • Permanent magnets on rotor (spinning part)
  • Electronic commutation (no brushes = longer life)
  • Requires 3-phase AC power from ESC

CRITICAL: Need 2 CW and 2 CCW motors (usually marked on base)

3. Electronic Speed Controllers - ESCs (4x) - ~$32-48 total

Rating: 30A continuous, 35A burst

Input: 2-4S LiPo (7.4V - 14.8V)

Function: Controls motor speed by converting DC to 3-phase AC

How It Works:

  1. Receives PWM signal from flight controller (1000-2000Œºs)
  2. Interprets signal as throttle percentage
  3. Rapidly switches DC power to motor's 3 phases
  4. Switching frequency determines motor speed

Connections:

  • 2 thick wires ‚Üí Battery (Red=+, Black=-)
  • 3 wires ‚Üí Motor (any order initially)
  • 3-pin servo connector ‚Üí Flight controller (Signal, +5V, GND)

BEC Feature: Most include 5V regulator to power flight controller

4. Flight Controller - ~$30-150

Options:

  • APM 2.8 - Budget option (~$35)
  • Pixhawk - Advanced option (~$100)
  • Arduino-compatible - KK2.1.5, MultiWii (~$30)

Functions:

  • Reads sensor data 100+ times per second
  • Runs PID control algorithms
  • Maintains flight stability
  • Executes pilot commands
  • Logs flight data

Key Sensors Inside:

  • Gyroscope (3-axis): Measures rotation rate (¬∞/sec)
  • Accelerometer (3-axis): Measures tilt and acceleration (g-forces)
  • Barometer: Measures altitude via air pressure
  • Magnetometer: Digital compass for heading
Flight Controller Loop (400 Hz):
1. Read sensors ‚Üí Current orientation
2. Compare to desired orientation
3. Calculate motor corrections (PID)
4. Send signals to ESCs
5. Repeat in 2.5ms

‚ö° Electronics Fundamentals

Understanding basic electronics is crucial for safely building and troubleshooting your drone.

Voltage, Current, and Resistance

Voltage (V) - Electrical "Pressure"

Measured in: Volts (V)

Analogy: Water pressure in a pipe

Example: 3S LiPo = 11.1V nominal

Current (I) - Flow of Electricity

Measured in: Amperes (Amps, A)

Analogy: Water flow rate

Example: Each motor draws 10-15A at full throttle

Resistance (R) - Opposition to Flow

Measured in: Ohms (Œ©)

Analogy: Pipe diameter (smaller = more resistance)

Example: Thicker wires have lower resistance

Ohm's Law

V = I √ó R
Voltage = Current √ó Resistance

Example:
Motor resistance: 0.5Œ©
Battery voltage: 11.1V
Current draw: V√∑R = 11.1V √∑ 0.5Œ© = 22.2A

Power Calculations

Power (P) - Energy per Second

P = V √ó I
Power = Voltage √ó Current
Measured in Watts (W)

Example:
Voltage: 11.1V
Current: 60A (4 motors √ó 15A each)
Power: 11.1V √ó 60A = 666W

Why This Matters:

  • Determines flight time
  • Battery must supply enough current
  • ESCs must handle the power
  • Wires must be thick enough

PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)

What is PWM? A method to control devices by varying pulse width, not voltage. 

Signal Period: 20ms (50Hz)
Pulse Width: 1000-2000 microseconds (Œºs)

ESC Interpretation:
1000Œºs = 0% throttle (motor off)
1500Œºs = 50% throttle (half speed)
2000Œºs = 100% throttle (full speed)

üî® Assembly Instructions

Step-by-step guide to building your F450 quadcopter. Take your time and double-check each step!

Pro Tip: Lay out all parts before starting. Check you have everything from the shopping list.

Step 1: Frame Assembly (20 minutes)

  • Identify all frame parts (upper/lower plates, 4 arms, landing gear)
  • Attach 4 arms to lower center plate (RED arms = FRONT)
  • Install landing gear on bottom of center plate
  • Attach upper plate using brass standoffs
  • Install motor mounts at end of each arm
  • Shake test - ensure all screws are tight

Step 2: Motor Installation (30 minutes)

  • Identify CW vs CCW motors (check labels)
  • Mount Motor 1 (Front Right - CW) to arm 1
  • Mount Motor 2 (Rear Left - CW) to arm 2
  • Mount Motor 3 (Front Left - CCW) to arm 3
  • Mount Motor 4 (Rear Right - CCW) to arm 4
  • Route motor wires along arms toward center
  • Secure wires with zip ties every 5cm
  • Test each motor spins freely by hand

Step 3: ESC Installation (40 minutes)

Soldering Required: This step requires careful soldering. Review safety procedures first!
  • Mount ESCs under arms (near motors) or in center stack
  • Strip and pre-tin ESC power wires (red/black thick wires)
  • Solder ESC power wires to PDB pads (integrated on frame)
  • Inspect solder joints (shiny, cone-shaped, no bridges)
  • Connect ESC output wires to motor wires (3 wires each)
  • Apply heat shrink to all connections
  • Secure ESCs with zip ties or velcro
Note: Motor direction will be tested and corrected later. Initial connection order doesn't matter.

Simple Motor Control Example

// Simple motor control with Arduino
#include <Servo.h>

Servo motor1, motor2, motor3, motor4;

void setup() {
  motor1.attach(3);
  motor2.attach(5);
  motor3.attach(6);
  motor4.attach(9);
  
  // Initialize to minimum throttle
  motor1.writeMicroseconds(1000);
  motor2.writeMicroseconds(1000);
  motor3.writeMicroseconds(1000);
  motor4.writeMicroseconds(1000);
  
  Serial.begin(115200);
}

void loop() {
  if (Serial.available()) {
    int throttle = Serial.parseInt();
    throttle = constrain(throttle, 1000, 2000);
    
    motor1.writeMicroseconds(throttle);
    motor2.writeMicroseconds(throttle);
    motor3.writeMicroseconds(throttle);
    motor4.writeMicroseconds(throttle);
    
    Serial.println("Throttle: " + String(throttle));
  }
}

PID Controller Basics

PID = Proportional-Integral-Derivative

The algorithm that keeps your drone stable!

P (Proportional): Response proportional to error

  • Big tilt = big correction
  • Too high P = oscillations

I (Integral): Eliminates steady-state error

  • Accumulates past errors
  • Corrects constant drift

D (Derivative): Dampens oscillations

  • Based on rate of change
  • Adds smoothness

First Flight

Pre-Flight Checklist

CRITICAL: Complete EVERY item before EVERY flight!
  • Flying in legal airspace (check FAA B4UFLY app)
  • Open area, 50m clearance minimum
  • Weather: Wind <15 mph, no rain
  • All screws tight (shake test)
  • Propellers installed correctly (CW/CCW)
  • No cracked or damaged props
  • Battery fully charged (>11.1V)
  • Battery secured (won't shift)
  • All connections secure
  • GPS lock (6+ satellites)
  • Radio connection strong
  • Flight mode: STABILIZE
  • Throttle at zero
  • Safety glasses on
  • Spotter present

Takeoff Procedure

  1. Final Safety Check: Everyone clear 5m minimum
  2. Power On: Transmitter FIRST, then connect battery
  3. Wait: 10-15 seconds for initialization
  4. ARM: Throttle stick to bottom-left, hold 3 seconds
  5. Takeoff: Slowly increase throttle to 60%
  6. Hover: Hold at 0.5-1 meter, stabilize
  7. Test Controls: Small stick inputs, verify response
  8. Landing: Slowly decrease throttle over 5 seconds
  9. Disarm: Throttle stick to bottom-right
  10. Power Off: Disconnect battery

First Flight Success Indicators

  • ‚úì All 4 motors spinning
  • ‚úì Drone lifts smoothly
  • ‚úì Hovers relatively stable
  • ‚úì Small corrections keep it steady
  • ‚úì Responds to stick inputs
  • ‚úì No unusual vibrations or sounds

Common First Flight Issues

Won't ARM

Check:

  • Throttle at zero?
  • GPS lock? (if required)
  • Calibrations complete?
  • Battery voltage sufficient?

Solution: Check Mission Planner "Messages" tab for specific error

Flips on Takeoff

Cause: Wrong motor direction/position

Solution:

  1. Cut throttle immediately!
  2. Disconnect battery
  3. Verify motor test results
  4. Check motor directions match diagram
  5. Swap 2 motor wires if needed

Drifts in One Direction

Causes: Accelerometer calibration, unbalanced weight, weak motor

Solution:

  • Recalibrate accelerometer on level surface
  • Adjust battery position for center of gravity
  • Inspect/replace motors if needed

üîß Troubleshooting Guide

Electrical Problems

No Power to Flight Controller

  • Battery charged? (>11.1V for 3S)
  • Battery connector correct polarity?
  • BEC from ESC working? (test with multimeter: 5V)
  • Power wires soldered correctly?
  • Check for short circuits

Motor Won't Spin

  • ESC connected to correct FC port?
  • ESC power wires connected to PDB?
  • Motor wires connected to ESC?
  • Motor physically jammed?
  • Try swapping with known good motor

Configuration Problems

GPS Won't Lock

  • Clear view of sky? (doesn't work indoors)
  • GPS powered? (LED blinking?)
  • Antennas not obstructed?
  • Away from metal/power lines?
  • Wait 5-10 minutes for first lock

Receiver Not Responding

  • Receiver bound to transmitter?
  • Receiver powered? (LED on?)
  • Transmitter turned on BEFORE drone?
  • Correct channel mapping?
  • Check RC input bars in Mission Planner

Mechanical Issues

Vibrations in Flight

Causes:

  • Unbalanced propellers
  • Damaged propellers
  • Loose motor mount
  • Bent motor shaft

Solutions:

  • Replace all props with new balanced set
  • Tighten all screws and mounts
  • Check motor shafts are straight
  • Add vibration dampening to FC
